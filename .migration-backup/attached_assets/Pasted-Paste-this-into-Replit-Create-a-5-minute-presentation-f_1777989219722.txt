Create a 5-minute presentation for my final year project app called RELIQ.

RELIQ is a discovery-first mobile marketplace for vintage fashion. It helps users discover one-of-one vintage pieces through a scrolling feed, while supporting independent vintage shops by giving them a digital sales channel.

The presentation should follow these 5 abstract points:

1. Broad research area
   Digital marketplaces and recommendation systems in vintage fashion.

2. Specific problem
   Vintage discovery online is inefficient. Existing platforms can become too specific after one search, creating repetitive feeds and limiting exploration.

3. Existing solutions and limitations
   Platforms like Vinted, Depop, and Etsy rely heavily on search or engagement-driven recommendations. These systems are often opaque, repetitive, and not designed to replicate the experience of browsing physical vintage racks.

4. Proposed solution
   RELIQ introduces a discovery-first mobile interface with:

* full-width product feed
* shop profiles that feel like browsing a rack
* Hidden Gems feed
* dynamic shopping persona system
* Style Influence, where users can let friends’ taste influence their feed
* product recommendations between users

5. Evaluation and outcomes
   The prototype is evaluated through functional testing and user interaction scenarios, focusing on discovery efficiency, usability, and perceived relevance. The expected outcome is that persona-driven and socially-influenced feeds improve exploration and support visibility for independent vintage shops.
